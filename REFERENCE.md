# Luna Llena — External Reference (award-grade, last ~18 months)

Per the Blocklist Law, the bar anchors ONLY to external award-grade reference, never
past builds. Researched fresh for THIS rebuild (the prior moonlit-coastal concept was
abandoned, not reused). The owner's note drove the brief: these are beautiful women
getting their hair done, so the site must be GORGEOUS, WARM, and ASPIRATIONAL. Someone
should land and immediately want to book.

## Sites studied (real, current, live)

1. **Serenity Hair, Blaxland** — https://serenityhairblaxland.com.au
   (Awwwards Nominee, Sept 2025). Soft warm-neutral palette pulled literally from the
   salon's hand-painted interior, natural-light photography, a hero line that sells a
   FEELING not a service. Takeaway: the **interior-extracted warm palette** — clay, sand,
   warm cream, deep cocoa text plus one saturated accent. Premium and welcoming, never
   clinical, never discount. This killed the old cold teal-night direction.

2. **ALICE Hair & Art, Stockholm** — https://www.alicehairart.se
   (Awwwards Honorable Mention, Feb 2025, Framer). Positions hairdressing AS art: the
   portfolio is framed like a curated gallery, not a before/after grid. Takeaway:
   **gallery-as-exhibition framing** — fewer, larger, matted, captioned-as-artworks
   tiles with one-at-a-time focus, which instantly elevates perceived value. We borrow
   the framing LOGIC, not its cool monochrome palette (we go warm).

3. **Warm-editorial salon class** — Awwwards Beauty gallery
   (https://www.awwwards.com/websites/beauty/) plus the warm-tone benchmarks Bendicion
   Salon and Artika referenced across 2025 salon-site roundups
   (https://thesalonbusiness.com/top-salon-websites/). Takeaway: **one consistent warm
   photographic grade** so a mixed set reads as a single editorial shoot, people-forward
   imagery that reflects the real clientele, and a Book action persistently reachable so
   desire converts the instant it spikes.

## What this build takes (the bar, not the shapes)
- A WARM GOLDEN moon, not a cold blue one: "Luna Llena" reframed as the harvest moon,
  the warm light you look your best under. Deep cocoa ground, warm cream, amber-gold glow.
- Editorial type contrast: a high-contrast display serif (Boska) against a clean
  grotesque (Switzer). Reads premium-magazine, not small-business template.
- Exhibition gallery of real, gorgeous, community-true salon work, one warm grade.
- A persistent, warm Book/Call action; desire built by calm editorial, captured at the peak.

## Cliche bans honored
NO pink florals, NO lotus clip art, NO Edwardian script, NO pastel gradient blobs,
NO discount-shop look, NO generic stock carousel, NO cold spa-grey. The warm golden
beauty-house direction is the deliberate anti-default for a Main St unisex salon.

## Signature (industry-rare)
A warm **golden moonglow** light source that drifts with the cursor (and on scroll on
touch), raking warm amber light across the work like the most flattering light in the
room finding you. "Te ves bien bajo cualquier luz" — you look good under any light, and
the best light is here. Reduced-motion-safe to a static warm glow.
